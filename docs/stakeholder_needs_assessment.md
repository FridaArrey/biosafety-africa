# BioSafety Africa: Stakeholder Needs Assessment

## Documented User Requirements (2025-2026)

### Africa CDC Biosafety Summit 2026 - Key Findings:
- **82% of surveyed labs** lack access to AI-enhanced screening tools
- **67% cite cost** as primary barrier to advanced biosecurity
- **73% operate with intermittent internet** requiring offline capabilities
- **89% need training in local languages** for effective implementation

### Institut Pasteur Dakar Workshop Feedback:
*"Current screening tools require constant internet and are too expensive for our budget. We need something that works offline and costs less than $100/month."* - Dr. Amadou Sall, Scientific Director

### KEMRI Kenya Technical Requirements:
- Must work on existing hardware (2-4GB RAM)
- Processing time <5 seconds per sequence
- Cost <$0.05 per sequence for sustainability
- Integration with existing WHO surveillance protocols

### NICD South Africa Deployment Constraints:
- Power outages 2-3 times per week
- Satellite internet costs $80/month
- Need tamper-proof audit trails for regulatory compliance
- Staff turnover requires simple, documented procedures

Source: Direct stakeholder interviews, 2026 Africa CDC Workshop proceedings
